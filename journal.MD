## Journal here!

So I thought of this cool idea because my brother was getting an Apple Watch and I thought to myself, what are the benefits of an overpriced watch? Nothing! A majority of people don't even use the watch to its full extent.

So I decided to make this project.

I am writing this after making the project with a stopwatch book I logged the hours into. (rounded to the nearest 10)

---

## Log 1

I loaded up the good old KiCad and laid every footprint I needed out. Then I pulled out my trusty old wiring tool and got to work. Obviously this was a basic rough outline without caps and resistors. I've done enough failed projects to know how to wire things at this point. It took me a solid 50 minutes to do everything.

**Time: 50 minutes**

---

## Log 2

I started reading datasheets and adding components 1 by 1. This took me a lot of time so I split this up into 2 logs. I looked through the power management, and the charging.

**Time: 2.30 hours**

---

## Log 3

After a night's rest I started again. I read and implemented the rest. It was really simple because they were just I2C components that needed decoupling caps and pull-up resistors.

**Time: 40 mins**

---

## Log 4

Well. There's the schematic. Done. I proceeded to go to Onshape like many have suggested and start designing. WOW it's great. But my old hunk of a laptop refused to have a smooth connection and the drops got to a point where I needed to switch to Tinkercad.

**Time: 7 hours gone..**

---

## Log 5

I was looking for a CMF watch vibe so I laid out the main base enclosure and threw a little plastic piece that sits as a bezel on top of the screen to keep it premium. Also while trying to calculate space which LUCKILY I did this case before the routing.

**Time: 3 hours**

---

## Log 6, 7, 8, 9, 10

Yes I took quite a lot of breaks because THIS was the hardest thing I've done.

With the space of a peanut I needed to implement ALL of this onto a 2 layer PCB. Thank god I took breaks.

I routed the USB differentials first then the rest. Surprisingly blindly routing everything then adjusting trace widths saved some time but I obviously needed to restart from scratch 9 times because of some traces that blocked other traces. It's like a puzzle. You need to find the order of things which are placed before you can place things.

**Time: 15 HOURS :skull:**

### Total, 29 Hours
